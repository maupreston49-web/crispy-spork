Mission Briefs

Calm Surrender

  • Our first priority is achieving a state of calm surrender in your dog: a regulated nervous system, loose body, and soft jaw. Only then will commands take hold.
  • We start with decompression walks and engagement games to reduce over‑arousal and build trust.
  • Your dog learns to accept guidance calmly, making future commands more effective.

Nose → Eyes → Ears

  • Dogs learn through their senses in a specific order. We begin with the nose (scent exploration), then progress to eyes (visual focus), and finally ears (verbal cues).
  • This sequence ensures your dog fully processes new environments and tasks, building confidence and trust.
  • By respecting this natural order, training becomes more intuitive and effective.

Trust → Respect → Love

  • We build relationships with dogs in the order of trust, respect, and love.
  • Trust is developed through calm leadership and consistent guidance.
  • Respect grows as your dog understands boundaries and expectations.
  • Love flourishes once trust and respect are established, leading to deep and lasting bonds.

To see these principles in action, choose a mission type that suits your dog’s drive and temperament.
